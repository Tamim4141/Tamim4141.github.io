Research

Thesis

Non‑destructive Evaluation of Concrete Compressive Strength Containing Steel Slag as Partially Replaced Coarse Aggregate

Summary: Experimental study combining Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity (UPV), Rebound Hammer, and compressive testing to establish correlations and predict compressive strength when steel slag replaces coarse aggregate. Explores optimum replacement percentage and proposes models for non‑destructive prediction.

Selected methods & tools

  • Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity (UPV)
  • Rebound Hammer testing
  • Compressive strength testing
  • Excel-based data analysis; simple statistical modeling

Developed linear and exponential correlations between UPV/Rebound Hammer values and compressive strength; identified ~50% slag replacement as optimal in tested mixes.
